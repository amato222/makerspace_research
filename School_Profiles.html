School

Small, Independent K-12

A private, tuition-based, K-12 school with academically rigorous curriculum, ample funding, and access to resources at a four-year research University. The school culture is defined by a commitment to the arts. This is reflected in the mission, the programming, as well as an on-campus gallery displaying work from the arts community.
Transition
Makerspace Leader. First school in the area to convert an existing computer lab into a makerspace. The transition was spearheaded by the technology teacher and by the Head of School, both committed to creativity and diversity.
Resources
Materials are ordered based on project needs, slowly accumulating over time
    Makerspace Mission
  • Provide hands-on experience designing and inventing.
  • Practice experimental application of STEM concepts.
  • Develop engineering roles through language and process
Makerspace Design
Open workshop with low wooden work tables and stools. Glass window invites students and teachers to observe and visit. Computers and 3D printers line the wall on one side.

Makerspace Model

Roles and Culture

Immersing learners in a culture of invention and collaboration. Students have opportunities to plan, experiment, and explain. They are encouraged to learn by breaking things, problem-solving,and persisting.They are encouraged to share knowledge, resources, and roles--to learn from each other.
    Programs by Grade-Level
  • Grades 3-8: Digital Design, Robotics, Science Olympiad
  • Grades 9-12: Applied Physics, Engineering
    • Roles in School System
    Play Space: Lower grades spend time at lunch and after school to play and create videogames or design models.
    Professional Development: Teachers from multiple disciplines come to learn and experiment with multimodal curriculum ideas.
    Technology Library: Teachers and students come to use 3D modeling programs and printers, and robotics materials.
    Guided Invention Lab: Novice engineering students engage in a culture of invention, learning the process and language of engineering.
    Student-Driven Invention Lab: Student pairs or teams choose a semester-long invention project.
