Participant Profiles
Role Development

Engineering Student

Inventor

Grades 9-12
A learner engaging predominately in problem-solving, iterative design, and strategy. The inventor communicates a point of view, is working on multiple project ideas, demonstrates competencies in procedural and conceptual understanding, and begins to form teams.
Key Social Role Exchanges
Producer-Evaluator, insider, problem-solver

Engineering Student

Enthusiast

Grades 9-12
A learner engaging predominately in problem-solving, iterative design, and strategy.The enthusiast chooses problem-solving or coaching roles more frequently, shows competencies in procedural skills, shows increases in conceptual understanding, and iniates more complex, meaningful projects.
Key Social Role Exchanges
Producer-Evaluator, insider, problem-solver

Engineering Student

Explorer

Grades 9-12
A learner engaging predominately in inquiry, experimentation, and research. The explorer chooses strategy and planning roles more frequently, shows evidence of metacognition, shows evidence of developing procedural knowledge, and attempts explanation construction.
Key Social Role Exchanges
Teacher-Student, Producer-Evaluator, Maker-Observer, Peer, Planner, Explainer.

Engineering Student

Participant

The participant chooses task-oriented roles most frequently, showing an ability to identify the immediate needs of a project, and collaborate. Participation in groups may involve defining new roles if task-oriented roles are taken, but this is often uncomfortable.
Key Social Role Exchanges
Insider-Outsider, Maker-Observer

Engineering Student

Novice

The novice shows a lack of confidence or understanding of how to participate in the project, and predominately chooses an observer role. There are few attempts at role exchange, and most exchanges occur from accepting the role assignments of others.
Key Social Role Exchanges
Insider-Outsider, Maker-Observer

Facilitator

Makerspace Director

Grades K-12
The director of the makerspace demonstrates commitments to developing creativity and diversity. The director is able to model role exchange and perspective-taking.
Maker Model
Interweaves constructionist learning model, invention with contrasting cases, apprenticeship model, and Teaching and Practice models.
Roles and Role Relationships
Co-teacher, collaborator, peer, master, expert, learner
The director is able to relate to and share about multiple disciplines and diverse communities with students. The director is confident identifying as a learner with respect to one or more disciplines practiced in the space. At the same time, the director demonstrates areas of expertise.

Visiting Teacher

Co-Teacher

Grades 6-8
Constructionist Model
This is a teacher that co-constructs spatial activities, so students can "build" a context for understanding new vocabulary or concepts. The teacher helps students with vocabulary or to understand relationships of structure and function during the activity. Students then choose five perspectives of their product for their presentation. The prize for most creative design and presentation is a physical 3D model of the student's design. The creativity that is possible in this activity is high, and it positively correlates with increases in learning.
Roles and Role Relationships
Student, observer, liner causal model

Visiting Teacher

Teacher Observer

Grades 4-8
A teacher who is a novice with respect to technology, and who relies heavily on the makerspace facilitator for the activity, for technical problem-solving, and for classroom managment.
Teaching and Practice Model
The teaching model is the most formal. It can only accomodate one role of teacher in a classroom. Because this teacher is a visitor and identifies as a novice with respect to technology, the role of teacher is assigned to the makerspace director, and a role of student or observer is assumed.
Roles and Role Relationships
Student, observer, liner causal model

Facilitator

Early-Career Engineer

Grades 9-12
The early-career engineer recently graduated from an undergraduate or graduate engineering program, and has little understanding of cognitive learning and teaching practices.
Apprenticeship Model
The primary cultural context that that is brought into the classroom space models professional engineering and technology environments. The engineer's team model outlines clear roles, to increase efficiency of production. Although efficiency make increase, role exchanges decrease.
Roles and Role Relationships
Project manager, expert

Facilitator

Undergraduate Engineering Student

Grades: 4-8
Active-Learning Model
Undergraduate engineering students from a four-year University coach science olympiad teams. There one or two mentors per 4 students. They coach the teams on the rules of the contest and attempt to help students discover and apply science concepts. They also are mindful of a production schedule that is needed to meet the deadline for the contest
Roles and Role Relationships
Student, observer, liner causal model
