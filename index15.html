About the Author

Anna Amato

M.A., Cognitive Studies in Education

Teachers College, Columbia University

New York, New York

My graduate-level research at Teachers College, Columbia University, has focused on evaluating cognitive development in school-based makerspace programs. Key areas of interest are systems reasoning, creativity, human-technology interaction, mathematical development, and metacognition. I have also designed activities to develop early systems reasoning skills using both traditional and digital tools, engaging students in math talk, causal explanations, and perspective-taking.

About the Research

I propose that perspective-taking, systems reasoning skills, and creative action are important criteria for evaluating whether a school-based maker program has established a culture of making. The projects described and modelled aim at building engineering skills and roles using a variety of construction materials. Creative technologies, in particular, provide opportunities to develop systems reasoning skills, engage in perspective-taking, and prepare students for the 21st century workplace. Many of the models on this website reflect patterns of role exchange and development in student collaborations during the design and construction of complex systems.
